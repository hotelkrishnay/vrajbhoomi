Essential FAQs About the Somnath Jyotirlinga Temple

Q: Where is the sacred Somnath Jyotirlinga Temple located in Gujarat?
The magnificent Somnath Temple is located in Prabhas Patan, near Veraval, right on the beautiful Arabian Sea coast in the Saurashtra region of Gujarat.
Q: What is the spiritual significance and history of Somnath, the First Jyotirlinga?
Somnath is profoundly significant as it is considered the First of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. Its current grand structure was completed in 1951, symbolizing resilience after being rebuilt several times throughout history.
Q: What are the current Somnath Temple darshan timings and Aarti schedule?
The Somnath Temple is open daily for darshan from 6:00 AM to 9:00 PM. The sacred Aarti is performed three times a day: Morning, Noon, and Evening.
Q: Is there an entry fee or ticket required for Somnath darshan?
No, there is absolutely no entry fee to enter the main Somnath Jyotirlinga Temple for darshan. A separate ticket is only required if you wish to attend the evening Light and Sound Show.
Q: Are mobile phones or cameras allowed inside the Somnath Temple complex?
No. For security and sanctity, mobile phones, cameras, and all other electronic devices are strictly not allowed inside the Somnath Temple premises. Secure lockers are available nearby.
Q: Can I attend the Light and Sound Show at Somnath Temple, and what time does it start?
Yes, the popular historical Light and Sound Show ("Jay Somnath") is held every evening, typically starting around 8:00 PM, showcasing the rich history of Somnath in a captivating display.
Q: What is the best time to visit Somnath Temple for comfortable weather?
The most pleasant time to plan your visit to Somnath is during the winter months, typically from October to March, when the weather is cool and ideal for darshan and local sightseeing.
Q: Is the Somnath Temple open every day, including weekends and holidays?
Yes, the Somnath Jyotirlinga Temple welcomes pilgrims every day of the week, including all weekends and public holidays, without exception.
Q: Is Somnath truly one of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva?
Yes, Somnath holds the prestigious position as the First among the 12 sacred Jyotirlingas (Luminaries) of Lord Shiva in India.
Q: Are there any specific rules or restrictions for foreigners visiting the Somnath Temple?
Foreign tourists are most welcome. They must adhere to the modest dress code and, like all visitors, are not allowed to carry cameras or phones inside the temple premises.
Q: Is there a formal dress code required for Somnath Temple darshan?
Yes, visitors are kindly requested to wear modest and traditional clothing. Attire such as shorts, short skirts, and sleeveless tops is not permitted inside the holy temple.
Q: Are lockers or luggage storage facilities available near Somnath Temple?
Yes, for the convenience of devotees, secure lockers and luggage storage facilities are readily available just outside the main Somnath Temple entrance.
Q: Can senior citizens or differently-abled persons get wheelchairs at Somnath Temple?
Yes, wheelchairs are provided at the temple premises to assist the elderly and differently-abled devotees with their Somnath darshan.
Q: How long does it usually take for darshan inside the Somnath Temple?
On regular weekdays, darshan usually takes between 30 to 45 minutes. Please be aware that during major festivals or weekends, the wait time may be significantly longer due to large crowds.
Q: What other shrines or attractions are part of the Somnath Temple Complex?
The sacred area includes the main temple, several smaller adjacent shrines, a dedicated museum, and the nearby holy site of Triveni Sangam (confluence of three rivers).
Q: Can pilgrims attend the morning Aarti at Somnath Temple?
Yes, the morning Aarti is a beautiful and peaceful experience and is open to all devotees, typically held around 7:00 AM (please confirm exact time locally).
Q: Are there VIP or special darshan passes available at Somnath Temple?
There is no formal VIP pass system. However, priority access in the queue is often given to senior citizens and individuals with special physical needs to facilitate their Somnath darshan.
Q: What is the official contact number for the Shree Somnath Temple Trust?
You can contact the Shree Somnath Trust directly at +91-2876-231212 for all official inquiries regarding darshan timings, temple programs, or donations.
Q: Can I make online donations to the Somnath Temple Trust?
Yes, for convenience, online donations can be securely processed through the official website of the Shree Somnath Trust using net banking, credit/debit cards, or UPI.
Q: Is prasad available for purchase after Somnath darshan?
Yes, prasad (sacred offering) is available for all devotees after their darshan and can be purchased from the designated counters near the exit of the Somnath Temple.
